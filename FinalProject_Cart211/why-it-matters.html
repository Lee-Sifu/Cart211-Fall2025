Why It Matters

Surveillance capitalism isn't just about annoying ads. It fundamentally reshapes power, democracy, autonomy, and human freedom. Here's why you should care.

2. Democracy at Risk

The Problem: Micro-targeted political advertising and algorithmic filter bubbles undermine informed democratic participation.

Cambridge Analytica: Used 87 million Facebook profiles to build psychological models and target swing voters with personalized political messages in the 2016 US election and Brexit referendum.

Filter Bubbles: Algorithms show you content that confirms your existing beliefs, creating polarized echo chambers where people can't agree on basic facts.

3. Insurance & Employment Discrimination

The Problem: Data brokers sell profiles that affect your insurance rates, loan approvals, and job prospects ‚Äî often based on factors you never consented to share.

Examples:

  • Health insurers buy data on your grocery purchases, gym visits, and online searches to adjust your premiums
  • Employers screen job candidates based on social media activity, location patterns, and behavioral predictions
  • Banks use browsing history and app usage to determine creditworthiness
  • Life insurance companies track your fitness data, sleep patterns, and daily routines

4. Loss of Anonymity & Free Expression

The Problem: When you know you're being watched, you self-censor. The chilling effect of surveillance changes how people think, speak, and act.

Research shows: People behave differently when they know they're being monitored. They conform more, take fewer risks, and express less controversial opinions.

Impact: Journalists, activists, whistleblowers, and ordinary people become vulnerable. Dissent becomes dangerous when every digital action is tracked and stored forever.
