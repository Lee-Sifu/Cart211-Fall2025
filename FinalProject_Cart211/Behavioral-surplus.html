Behavioral Surplus Explained

Behavioral surplus refers to the extra data that companies collect beyond what is necessary to improve a service. Google discovered that this surplus could be used to predict and shape user behavior — forming the backbone of surveillance capitalism.

Every keystroke, location ping, or voice command becomes part of a hidden feedback loop: collected, analyzed, and sold to advertisers eager to anticipate your next move. The more you interact online, the richer the data supply becomes.

Understanding behavioral surplus helps reveal the imbalance of power between users and platforms — a one-way mirror where your life is the content, and the market is always watching.
