Reclaim Your Digital Autonomy

You can't completely escape surveillance capitalism while using the internet, but you can significantly reduce your exposure. Here are practical steps organized by difficulty level.

Your Current Privacy Protection

15% Protected

Average internet user starts here. Let's improve it.

üü¢ EASY - Start Here (30 minutes)

Quick Wins for Immediate Privacy Improvement

  • Switch to Firefox or Brave browser (better privacy than Chrome)
  • Install uBlock Origin extension (blocks trackers and ads)
  • Switch default search engine to DuckDuckGo or Startpage
  • Review and limit app permissions on your phone
  • Turn off ad personalization in Google and Facebook settings
  • Use incognito/private mode for sensitive searches
  • Clear cookies regularly or set browser to auto-delete

Impact: Blocks 60-70% of common trackers immediately. Minimal effort, significant gain.

üü° MEDIUM - Worth the Effort (1-2 hours)

Stronger Protection for Motivated Users

  • Use a VPN (Virtual Private Network) for all browsing - try ProtonVPN or Mullvad
  • Switch to Signal for messaging (end-to-end encrypted by default)
  • Use ProtonMail or Tutanota for email (encrypted email services)
  • Install a privacy-focused operating system on phone (LineageOS, GrapheneOS for advanced users)
  • Use password manager (Bitwarden, 1Password) with unique passwords for every site
  • Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) everywhere possible
  • Delete unused accounts and request data deletion under GDPR/CCPA
  • Audit your Google and Facebook data downloads to see what they have

Impact: Significantly reduces corporate surveillance. Your ISP and apps can still track you, but much less data leaks.

üî¥ ADVANCED - Maximum Privacy (Ongoing commitment)

For Those Serious About Digital Freedom

  • Use Tor Browser for anonymous browsing (routes traffic through multiple nodes)
  • Run your own VPN server or use privacy-focused hosting
  • De-Google your life completely - migrate all services
  • Use Linux operating system instead of Windows or macOS
  • Pay with cryptocurrency or privacy-focused payment methods
  • Use separate devices for different activities (compartmentalization)
  • Regularly audit your digital footprint and request deletions
  • Support privacy legislation and digital rights organizations
  • Educate others about surveillance capitalism

Impact: Near-maximum privacy. Requires lifestyle changes and ongoing maintenance. Not for everyone, but available for those who need it.

‚ö†Ô∏è Important Reality Check

Perfect privacy doesn't exist. Even with all these tools:

  • Your ISP can still see your traffic (VPN helps but isn't perfect)
  • If you use Google/Meta services, they'll still track you on their platforms
  • Phone carriers track your location
  • Data brokers may already have years of your data
  • Government surveillance operates at a different level

Goal: Reduce your surveillance footprint and make tracking expensive. Force them to work for it.

Your Legal Rights

üá™üá∫ GDPR (Europe)

Your rights:

  • Right to access your data
  • Right to deletion ("right to be forgotten")
  • Right to data portability
  • Right to object to processing

How to use it: Email companies directly requesting data deletion under GDPR Article 17.

üá∫üá∏ CCPA (California)

Your rights:

  • Right to know what data is collected
  • Right to delete personal information
  • Right to opt out of data sales
  • Right to non-discrimination

How to use it: Look for "Do Not Sell My Personal Information" links on websites.

Apps to Audit & Delete

High-Risk Apps (Consider Deleting)

  • TikTok: Extreme data collection, potential foreign surveillance
  • Facebook App: Tracks even when closed, uses microphone. Use browser instead.
  • Most free VPN apps: They're free because they sell your data
  • Flashlight apps: No reason to need location/contacts for a flashlight
  • Period tracking apps (non-encrypted): Sell health data to brokers
  • Free games with excessive permissions: Why does Candy Crush need your contacts?

The Most Important Step

Digital minimalism: The less you share, the less they can take.
The fewer services you use, the smaller your attack surface.
The more conscious your choices, the more control you have.

Start with one change today. Build from there.
