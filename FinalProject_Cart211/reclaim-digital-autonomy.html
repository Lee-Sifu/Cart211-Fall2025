Reclaim Your Digital Autonomy

While the problem is systemic, individual actions matter. Here are concrete, practical steps you can take today to reduce your digital footprint, protect your data, and reclaim your autonomy.

15% Protected

Immediate Actions (Do These Today)

1. Change Privacy Settings Everywhere

Facebook/Instagram: Privacy → Audience → Change to "Friends Only"

Google: myaccount.google.com → Privacy → Activity Controls → Turn off

Twitter/X: Privacy and safety → Make account private

Time required: 30 minutes

2. Clear Cookies and Browsing History

Chrome: Settings → Privacy and security → Clear browsing data → Select "Cookies" and "Cached images"

Firefox: Preferences → Privacy & Security → Cookies and Site Data → Clear Data

Safari: Preferences → Privacy → Manage Website Data → Remove All

Important: Do this regularly (weekly or monthly)

Time required: 5 minutes

3. Disable Location Services

iPhone: Settings → Privacy → Location Services → Toggle OFF (or set to "While Using")

Android: Settings → Location → Turn OFF (or use app-specific permissions)

Google Timeline: myaccount.google.com → Location History → Turn OFF

Time required: 5 minutes

4. Review App Permissions

iPhone: Settings → Apps → Check which apps have access to Camera, Microphone, Location, Photos, Contacts

Android: Settings → Apps → App Permissions → Review access

Delete or restrict: Apps that ask for excessive permissions they don't need

Time required: 15 minutes

Browser-Level Protection

Install uBlock Origin

What it does: Blocks third-party trackers and ad networks

Install on: Chrome, Firefox, Safari

Cost: Free

Impact: Significantly reduces tracking, blocks malware ads

Install Privacy Badger

What it does: Learns which trackers are following you and blocks them

Install on: Chrome, Firefox

Cost: Free

Impact: Smart tracking prevention, actively learns from your browsing

Switch to Privacy-Focused Browser

Options:

  • Firefox: Built-in tracking protection, no data collection by default
  • Brave: Blocks ads and trackers by default, built-in VPN option
  • DuckDuckGo: Doesn't track searches, open-source privacy

Impact: Fundamental reduction in tracking from the browser level

Email & Account Management

Switch to Privacy-Focused Email

Options:

  • ProtonMail: End-to-end encrypted, based in Switzerland (strong privacy laws)
  • Tutanota: Encrypted by default, open-source
  • Hey.com: Blocks trackers in emails, paid service ($99/year)

Why: Gmail scans your emails for behavioral data. Switching protects your communications.

Use a Password Manager

Options: Bitwarden (free, open-source), 1Password, KeePass

Benefits: Create unique passwords for every site, harder to track across sites

Important: Avoid LastPass (had data breaches)

Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

How: Account Settings → Security → Enable 2FA

Use: Authenticator app (Authy, Google Authenticator) rather than SMS when possible

Accounts: Email, social media, banking, any important account

Advanced Protection (More Technical)

Use a VPN

What it does: Encrypts your traffic and hides your IP address from websites and ISPs

Recommended: Mullvad, ProtonVPN, IVPN

Cost: Most charge $5-10/month

Important: Avoid free VPNs (they sell your data to pay the bills)

Change Your DNS Provider

What it does: Prevents ISP from seeing which websites you visit

Options: Quad9, NextDNS, or your VPN provider's DNS

How: Router settings or OS network settings

Impact: ISP can't see your browsing history

Consider Linux

What it is: Free, open-source operating system that doesn't collect your data

Recommended distros: Ubuntu, Fedora, or Debian for beginners

Impact: Eliminate surveillance from your OS level

Barrier: Steeper learning curve, but worth it

Behavioral Changes

Reduce Social Media Use

Delete Facebook, Instagram, TikTok, or set strict limits on usage. These platforms are built on behavioral surplus extraction.

Alternative: Use mastodon.social or Bluesky (decentralized social networks with no behavioral data extraction)

Use Privacy-Focused Search Engines

Options: DuckDuckGo, Startpage, Searx

Benefit: Your searches aren't tracked and stored in a profile about you

Trade-off: Results may be slightly less personalized (which is good!)

Avoid "Sign in With Google/Facebook"

Every time you use these services to sign into other apps, you're giving them behavioral data about your activity across the web.

Alternative: Create separate accounts on each service

Minimize Personal Information Shared Online

Every detail you post contributes to your behavioral profile. Think twice before sharing:

  • Photos (Information visible in photos, like a home address or license plate)
  • Location tags
  • Relationship status
  • Religious/political beliefs
  • Health information

Know Your Rights

GDPR (Europe)

If you live in the EU, you have the right to:

  • Know what data companies collect about you
  • Request a copy of your data (Data Subject Access Request)
  • Delete your data (Right to be Forgotten)
  • Opt-out of data selling

Action: Send a DSAR to any company. They must respond in 30 days.

CCPA (California)

If you live in California, you have the right to:

  • Know what data is collected
  • Delete your personal information
  • Opt-out of the sale of your data

Action: Use privacy.ca.gov to submit requests

Systemic Change Requires Advocacy

Support Privacy Organizations

Organizations like the Electronic Frontier Foundation (EFF), Center for Democracy & Technology (CDT), and Mozilla Foundation are fighting for digital rights.

Donate, volunteer, or sign petitions to support their work.

Demand Better Laws

Contact your elected representatives and demand stronger privacy laws, regulation of data brokers, and accountability for surveillance capitalism.

Write to your senators, representatives, and local officials.

Educate Others

Share this website with friends, family, and colleagues. Talk about surveillance capitalism. Make it visible.

The problem thrives in darkness. Visibility is the first step to change.

Remember

Individual privacy protection is important, but it's not enough. Surveillance capitalism is a systemic problem that requires systemic solutions. Keep fighting for legislative change while protecting yourself in the short term.
