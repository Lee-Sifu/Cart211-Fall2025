What Is Surveillance Capitalism?

Coined by Shoshana Zuboff, surveillance capitalism describes an economic system that claims human experience as free raw material for extraction, prediction, and profit.

Companies like Google and Meta collect massive amounts of behavioral data from users. These data are processed through machine learning systems to predict and influence future behavior — the foundation of a trillion-dollar digital economy.

Unlike traditional capitalism, where goods are exchanged for money, here our private moments become the raw material for a new kind of market: the trade in human futures.

Key Characteristics

Data Extraction

Every interaction online is recorded and stored. Your searches, clicks, locations, contacts, and even your thoughts (typed but not sent) are all collected and monetized.

Behavioral Prediction

Machine learning algorithms analyze your data to predict what you will do next. These predictions are then sold to advertisers and used to influence your behavior.

Behavioral Modification

Once companies can predict your behavior, they can influence it. Through targeted ads, personalized feeds, and algorithmic recommendations, they shape what you see, think, and do.

"Surveillance capitalism unilaterally claims experience as raw material for behavioral predictions. As the surplus of behavioral data has grown and as the economic value of predictions has become clear, we are now facing unprecedented circumstances in the history of capitalism." — Shoshana Zuboff

How Is It Different From Traditional Capitalism?

Aspect Traditional Capitalism Surveillance Capitalism
Raw Material Natural resources, labor, goods Human experience and behavior
Product Sold Physical or digital goods Predictions about your future behavior
Consumer You buy the product Advertisers buy predictions about you
Transparency You know what you're buying You don't know you're the product
Consent You choose to participate Participation is forced or hidden

A Brief History

1998

Google Founded

Google begins as a search engine. It discovers that user search behavior is incredibly valuable and starts collecting this data at massive scale.

2000s

Targeted Advertising Emerges

Companies realize behavioral data can predict which ads will work on which people. Targeted advertising becomes the primary business model.

2004

Facebook Launches

Facebook takes surveillance capitalism to new heights by collecting not just behavior, but relationships, opinions, and personal information.

2007

Smartphone Revolution

Mobile devices are tracking us 24/7, including our location, who we talk to, and where we go. Surveillance becomes ubiquitous.

2024-Present

Peak Surveillance

AI and machine learning have made behavioral prediction incredibly accurate. Tech companies know you better than you know yourself.
