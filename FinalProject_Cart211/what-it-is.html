What Is Surveillance Capitalism?

Coined by Shoshana Zuboff, surveillance capitalism describes an economic system that claims human experience as free raw material for extraction, prediction, and profit.

Companies like Google and Meta collect massive amounts of behavioral data from users. These data are processed through machine learning systems to predict and influence future behavior — the foundation of a trillion-dollar digital economy.

Unlike traditional capitalism, where goods are exchanged for money, here our private moments become the raw material for a new kind of market: the trade in human futures.
