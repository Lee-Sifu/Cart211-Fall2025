How They Track You

Every moment online, invisible systems are collecting data about your behavior. Here are the primary methods used to surveil your digital life.

The Tracking Methods (Hover to Reveal)

Cookies & Third-Party Trackers

Small text files stored on your device that track your browsing behavior across different websites. Third-party cookies follow you from site to site, building a complete profile of your interests and habits.

üëÜ

Browser Fingerprinting

Companies collect data about your device (OS, browser, screen resolution, installed fonts) to create a unique "fingerprint" that identifies you without cookies or login credentials.

üìç

Pixel Tags & Beacons

Invisible 1x1 pixel images embedded in emails and websites that notify companies when you open an email or visit a page. Used to track open rates and validate pixel-level user engagement.

üìç

Location Tracking

GPS data, Wi-Fi signals, and cell tower triangulation combine to track your physical location. Apps, advertisers, and governments can determine where you are at any moment.

How to See Who's Tracking You

üîç Open your browser's Developer Tools (F12) and check the Network Tab

Step 1: Press F12 to open Developer Tools

Step 2: Go to the "Network" tab

Step 3: Reload the page

Step 4: Look for requests to domains you don't recognize (these are trackers!)

You'll likely see requests to Google Analytics, Facebook Pixel, and dozens of ad networks.
