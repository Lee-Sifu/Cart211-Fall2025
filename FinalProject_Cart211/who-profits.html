Who Profits From Your Data

A handful of tech giants and thousands of data brokers have built trillion-dollar empires on the foundation of human behavioral data. Here's who's cashing in on surveillance capitalism.

The Big Players

Google

Annual Revenue: $307 Billion (2023)

Profit From Data: ~$180 Billion (59%) from advertising

What They Track: Every Google search, YouTube video watched, Gmail contents, location via Google Maps, and Android devices. Alphabet owns over 90% of global search and has visibility into the vast majority of web traffic.

Learn More About Google's Data Collection

Meta (Facebook, Instagram, WhatsApp)

Annual Revenue: $134 Billion (2023)

Profit From Data: ~$131 Billion (98%) from advertising

What They Track: Your social relationships, content preferences, photos, messages (via WhatsApp in many countries), and location. Meta's business model is entirely built on profiling users and selling targeting to advertisers.

Learn More About Meta's Data Collection

Amazon

Annual Revenue: $575 Billion (2023)

Profit From Data: AWS services and advertising growing rapidly (~$15B+)

What They Track: Your purchase history, browsing behavior, searches on their site, Alexa voice commands, and increasingly, real-world behavior through Ring cameras and cashier-less stores.

Learn More About Amazon's Data Collection
