Resources & Credits

Want to learn more? Here are the essential resources for understanding surveillance capitalism and protecting your digital rights.

Books & Academic Resources

"The Age of Surveillance Capitalism"
Shoshana Zuboff (2019)

The definitive book on surveillance capitalism. Zuboff introduces the term and explores how Google discovered this business model, starting with "behavioral surplus."

Free PDF
"The Master Switch"
Tim Wu (2010)

Historical analysis of how power in telecommunications has shifted between different platforms. Explains why monopolies form and how they abuse power.

Must Read
"Weapons of Math Destruction"
Cathy O'Neil (2016)

Explores how algorithms perpetuate bias and discrimination in criminal justice, hiring, lending, and education. Essential reading on algorithmic harm.

Must Read

Documentaries & Films

"The Social Dilemma"
Netflix (2020)

Explores how social media algorithms are manipulating users through interviews with former tech executives. Accessible introduction to the topic.

"Coded Bias"
Netflix (2020)

Investigates bias in facial recognition technology and how algorithms discriminate against women and people of color.

"The Great Hack"
Netflix (2019)

Explores the Cambridge Analytica scandal and how personal data was weaponized for political campaigns.

Privacy Tools & Services

VPN Services

What they do: Hide your IP address and encrypt your internet traffic

  • Mullvad: No registration, excellent privacy (mullvad.net)
  • ProtonVPN: From the creators of ProtonMail (protonvpn.com)
  • IVPN: Privacy-focused, transparent (ivpn.io)

Browser Extensions

What they do: Block trackers, ads, and malicious scripts

  • uBlock Origin: Powerful ad and tracker blocker (free, open-source)
  • Privacy Badger: Learns and blocks trackers automatically
  • HTTPS Everywhere: Forces encrypted connections
  • Decentraleyes: Blocks CDNs that track you

Private Email Providers

What they do: Encrypted email that doesn't scan your messages

  • ProtonMail: End-to-end encrypted, based in Switzerland (protonmail.com)
  • Tuta(Tutanota): Encrypted by default, open-source (tuta.com)
  • Zoho Mail: Privacy-respecting alternative (zoho.com)

Private Search Engines

What they do: Don't track your searches

  • DuckDuckGo: Privacy by default (duckduckgo.com)
  • Startpage: Private Google search results (startpage.com)
  • SearXNG: Metasearch engine, decentralized (searXNG.org)

Privacy-Focused Operating Systems

What they do: Don't collect data at the OS level

  • Linux: Free, open-source alternative to Windows/Mac
  • Tails: Amnesic Incognito Live System (extreme privacy, leaves no trace)
  • Whonix: Anonymity-focused Linux distribution

Organizations Advocating for Digital Rights

Electronic Frontier Foundation (EFF)
www.eff.org

Leading nonprofit defending digital privacy, free speech, and innovation. Fights for user rights against surveillance and corporate abuse.

Center for Democracy & Technology (CDT)
www.cdt.org

Works to advance civil liberties in digital space. Advocates for strong privacy laws and digital rights.

Mozilla Foundation
www.mozilla.org

Creates privacy-respecting browser (Firefox) and advocates for digital rights and internet health.

Privacy International
www.privacyinternational.org

Global organization defending the right to privacy. Conducts research on surveillance and fights for privacy rights worldwide.

Access Now
www.accessnow.org

Defends and extends the digital rights of users at risk around the world. 24/7 digital security response center.
