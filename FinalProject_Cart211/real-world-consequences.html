Target's Pregnancy Prediction Algorithm

What happened: Target's algorithm predicted a teenage girl's pregnancy before her father knew by analyzing her shopping patterns.

Early 2012

Target developed predictive analytics to identify pregnant customers by tracking purchases of unscented lotion, supplements, and other products.

February 2012

A teenager's purchasing behavior triggered the algorithm. Target began sending her coupons for baby products and maternity clothes.

Days Later

Her father discovered the coupons, went to Target to complain, accusing them of encouraging teen pregnancy.

The Truth

Cambridge Analytica & Election Manipulation

What happened: Cambridge Analytica harvested 87 million Facebook profiles without consent and used the data to psychologically manipulate voters in the 2016 US election and Brexit referendum.

87 Million

Facebook users whose data was stolen

How they did it:

  • Created a personality quiz app on Facebook that collected data from users AND their friends
  • Built psychological profiles predicting personality traits, political views, and vulnerabilities
  • Used profiles to micro-target voters with personalized political ads designed to manipulate behavior
  • Tested thousands of ad variations to find which messages would be most effective at changing minds
5,000+

Data points collected per person to create psychological manipulation profiles

The consequences:

Democracy Undermined Mass Manipulation Election Interference Unauthorized Data Harvesting

The outcome: Facebook was fined $5 billion by the FTC in 2019. Cambridge Analytica shut down, but the playbook still exists and is being used today.

SYSTEMIC IMPACT

Price Discrimination

Airlines, hotels, and e-commerce sites show different prices to different users based on their browsing history, location, and device type.

  • Mac users shown higher prices than Windows users
  • Prices increase if you've searched multiple times (showing urgency)
  • Higher prices shown to users in wealthier zip codes
  • Dynamic pricing based on your perceived willingness to pay

Result: You pay more for the same product because algorithms determined you would.

SYSTEMIC IMPACT

Job Application Discrimination

AI hiring systems screen candidates based on patterns in training data, often replicating historical biases.

  • Amazon's hiring AI taught itself to penalize resumes with the word "women's"
  • Algorithms reject candidates from certain zip codes
  • Video interview AIs judge "personality" based on facial expressions, accents, background settings
  • Social media activity used to screen without candidate knowledge

Result: Qualified candidates rejected by opaque algorithms. No explanation, no appeal, no recourse.

The Pattern
